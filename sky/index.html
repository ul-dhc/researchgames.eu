About

It started with a conversation.

ResearchGames.eu started with a conversation between two researchers.

Sanita studies folklore, digital humanities and cultural heritage in Rīga. Alex studies the molecular structures of proteins and DNA in Brussels. At their core, our fields seem to have little in common.

Talking about our work meant going back to the beginning: What are you looking for? How do you know when you have found it? Trying to answer those questions for each other led us to wonder whether research could be shared in a more active way.

Could research be
experienced as a game?

We thought it could – if a game did more than explain the result and allowed players to take part in the thinking. That is what ResearchGames.eu sets out to do. Together with researchers, we turn publications, datasets and research practices into games that people can explore for themselves.

University of Latvia

The University of Latvia made ResearchGames.eu possible, enabling an interdisciplinary idea to become the first international platform dedicated to turning academic research into web games. Developed at the UL Digital Humanities Center, the platform brings researchers and game design together to make research playable and advance innovative forms of science communication.
